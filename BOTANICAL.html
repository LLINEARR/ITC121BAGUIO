What is the Botanical Garden?

The Baguio Botanical Garden, renowned for its diverse collection of plants, trees, and flowers, making it a popular destination for nature enthusiasts, tourists, and locals alike. Established as a sanctuary for various plant species indigenous to the Cordillera region, the garden also features landscaped areas, walking paths, and picturesque spots perfect for leisurely strolls and relaxation. Visitors can explore themed gardens, such as the herbal garden, and enjoy the tranquil ambiance amidst lush greenery. Additionally, the Baguio Botanical Garden serves as an educational venue, providing information about the region's flora and promoting environmental awareness.

HISTORY BACKGROUND

Botanical Garden

The Baguio Botanical Garden in the Philippines traces its roots back to the early 1900s when it was established as a small area for cultivating native plant species. Originally conceived as a hub for research and conservation, it gradually evolved into a public garden, welcoming visitors to explore its diverse flora and fauna. Over the years, it has undergone expansions and renovations, incorporating themed gardens and educational exhibits to enhance its appeal to tourists and locals alike. Today, the Baguio Botanical Garden stands as a testament to the rich biodiversity of the region, serving as both a recreational space and a vital center for environmental education and conservation efforts in the Philippines.

The Botanical Garden

This verdant haven invites visitors to wander through lush gardens, meandering pathways, and discover the rich biodiversity of the Philippines. From its fragrant orchids to its towering ferns, each corner unveils a tapestry of natural wonders, while the traditional Igorot Village offers a glimpse into indigenous heritage. Immerse yourself in nature's beauty and cultural heritage at the Baguio Botanical Garden, where every step is a journey of discovery and tranquility.
