What is Burnham Park?

Burnham Park stands out as one of Baguio City's most visited destinations for its captivating scenery, offering a serene retreat amidst the city's hustle and bustle. Named after the visionary architect Daniel Hudson Burnham, the park holds both historical significance and natural charm. Its picturesque landscapes, including lush greenery and tranquil bodies of water, provide a perfect backdrop for a variety of recreational activities. From boating on the lake to leisurely strolls along its pathways, visitors of all ages can find enjoyment. Situated in the heart of the city, Burnham Park serves as a central hub for cultural events, festivals, and family gatherings, welcoming both locals and tourists alike to experience its beauty and ambiance. With its family-friendly environment and diverse offerings, Burnham Park continues to enchant visitors seeking relaxation and outdoor enjoyment in Baguio City.

HISTORY BACKGROUND

Daniel Burnham

Daniel Burnham lies in the park's name. Burnham was commissioned by the American colonial government to develop plans for the city of Baguio in the early 1900s. His vision for Baguio included wide roads, parks, and open spaces designed to enhance the city's beauty and livability. While Burnham himself did not design Burnham Park specifically, the park was named in his honor as a tribute to his role in planning the city. Thus, the connection between Daniel Burnham and Burnham Park lies in his broader influence on the urban development of Baguio City during the American colonial period.
