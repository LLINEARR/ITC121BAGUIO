What is The Mansion?

The Baguio Mansion, officially known as The Mansion House, is the official summer residence of the President of the Philippines. Located in Baguio City, it sits on a vast estate surrounded by pine trees and beautifully landscaped gardens. The Mansion's architectural style is reminiscent of American colonial-era structures, with its distinctive white facade and neo-classical design.

HISTORY BACKGROUND

The Mansion

Originally constructed in 1908 during the American colonial period, serves as the official summer residence of the President of the Philippines. Initially intended for American Governor-Generals seeking relief from the lowland heat, it hosted significant political and social events. During World War II, it was occupied by Japanese forces before being turned over to the Philippine government. Since then, it has been a retreat for Philippine presidents and their families during vacations in Baguio. Apart from its residential function, The Mansion has also hosted official functions and ceremonies, symbolizing Baguio's heritage and attracting tourists with its historic charm and picturesque setting.
