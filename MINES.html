What is Mines View Park?

Mines View Park is one of Baguio's most iconic attractions, offering breathtaking panoramic views of the surrounding mountains and the abandoned gold and copper mines of Benguet province. Visitors can enjoy stunning vistas of the Cordillera mountain range, dotted with pine trees and terraced hillsides. The park is adorned with indigenous Igorot huts and is a popular spot for taking photos against the backdrop of the scenic landscape. Additionally, it features souvenir shops where visitors can purchase traditional handicrafts, jewelry, and other mementos. Mines View Park provides a serene escape from the bustling city, allowing visitors to appreciate the natural beauty and cultural heritage of the region

HISTORY BACKGROUND

Mines View Park

Mines View Park holds historical significance dating back to the early 1900s when the American colonial government established mining operations in the region. Baguio and its surrounding areas, including Benguet province, became centers of mining activity due to the discovery of gold and copper deposits. During the American colonial period, which began in the late 19th century, mining became a major industry in the Philippines, particularly in the Cordillera region where Baguio is located. Mines View Park sits atop one of the hills overlooking the mines, providing a strategic vantage point for overseeing the mining operations.
